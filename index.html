🎫 Needs Reservation
🔲
Pearl Harbor / USS Arizona Memorial
Book 60 days ahead — ASAP
Most emotionally powerful site in Hawaii. Budget 3–4 hrs. Includes boat ride to the memorial over the sunken ship. Technically free but sells out — standby line = 2–3 hr wait.
recreation.gov · Free + $1 fee
🔲
Diamond Head Crater Hike
Book 30 days ahead — Aug 2
1.8-mile hike, 560 ft elevation. Book 7–8 AM slot — cooler, golden light, fewer crowds. Zero shade on the trail — sunscreen, hat, water essential.
reserveamerica.com · $5/person + $10/vehicle
🔲
Hanauma Bay Snorkel
Book 2 days ahead — Aug 26 @ 1 PM Columbus
Protected volcanic crater bay — 400+ fish species. Best snorkel on Oahu. Tickets drop at 7 AM HST sharp and sell out in minutes. Closed Monday & Tuesday — plan for Wed–Sun. Arrive early, parking fills by 8 AM.
dlnr.hawaii.gov/hanauma · $25/person

🔲
Haleakalā Sunrise
60-day window — Jul 4 @ 1 PM Columbus
Above-the-clouds at 10,023 ft. One of the most surreal experiences in Hawaii. 40–50°F at summit — jacket, gloves, hat required. Arrive 30+ min before sunrise. After sunrise, stay for the Sliding Sands Trail crater hike (2–4 hrs extra).
recreation.gov · $1 + $30/vehicle
🔲
Wai'anapanapa Black Sand Beach
30-day window — Aug 1 @ 1 PM Columbus
Jet-black volcanic sand, sea caves, lava arches. One of Maui's most photogenic spots. Book the 7 AM slot for best dramatic light and fewest people.
gostateparks.hawaii.gov · $5/person + $10/vehicle

Molokini Crater Snorkel
Book ASAP — fills weeks ahead
Submerged volcanic crater 3 miles offshore. 150-ft visibility, 250+ fish species. One of the world's top snorkel sites. Half-day catamaran departing Maalaea Harbor ~7 AM. Pack a light jacket for the ocean breeze.
Trilogy Excursions (premium, smaller boats): sailtrilogy.com

🐠 Beaches & Snorkelling
🔲
Makena Beach (Big Beach)
No reservation · Free
3,000 ft of golden sand, crystal-clear water. One of Maui's largest and most beautiful beaches. Powerful shore break — don't turn your back on the waves.
🔲
Kapalua Beach
No reservation · Free
Protected northwest Maui cove. Calm, beautiful, good snorkelling near rocky edges. Turtle sightings very common. Quieter than south Maui.
🔲
Honolua Bay
No reservation · No facilities
Raw and rugged. Outstanding coral reef — one of Maui's best snorkel spots. Accessed via a short dirt trail. Bring everything you need.
🔲
Keawakapu Beach (Kihei)
No reservation · Free
Soft sand, calm clear water, minimal crowds. 20-min drive from Andaz. Great for morning walks and easy snorkelling near rocky outcrops.

All-day drive: 64 miles, 620 curves, 59 bridges. Leave Andaz by 6:30–7 AM to stay ahead of traffic. Stops: Wailua Falls, Aunty Sandy's banana bread, Wai'anapanapa, Hana town. Consider the full loop back via Ulupalakua to avoid backtracking.

Wailua Falls
No reservation · Roadside stop
Stunning 80-ft twin falls. Easy roadside stop on Road to Hana — no hike required.
🔲
Ho'okipa Beach Park
No reservation · Free
World-famous windsurfing beach. 40+ green sea turtles haul out on the beach in the evening (after ~3 PM). Perfect stop when visiting Paia.
🔲
Nakalele Blowhole
Volcanic blowhole shooting water 50+ ft. Dramatic north Maui coastal scenery. Moderate 20-min hike. Don't get too close — sneaker waves are dangerous.

🚗 Rental Car — Maui
Pick up at OGG on Aug 31 arrival. Book NOW — Maui cars sell out in summer. Budget/Alamo/National tend to have best rates.

Get a standard SUV (not economy) — roads to Haleakalā and parts of the Hana Hwy benefit from extra clearance. Most rental agreements prohibit driving Pi'ilani Hwy 31 (back road of Hana) — check your policy before that loop.

Rental car is not needed on O'ahu — Waikiki is walkable; Uber/Lyft fine for Pearl Harbor, Hanauma Bay, etc.
🕐 Timezone
HST is always UTC−10 (no daylight saving) Columbus (EDT) in August = UTC−4. So HST = Columbus − 6 hours. A 7 AM HST booking window opens at 1 PM Columbus time. Set alarms 10 min early.
🥗 Vegetarian Tips
Best veg hubs O'ahu: Downtown Honolulu and Ala Moana area — many Japanese, Indian, health-food options. Avoid tourist traps on Kalakaua Ave (Waikiki main strip) — walk one block inland for better food and lower prices.

Maui: Paia Town (North Shore) is the jackpot — Café Mambo, Flatbread Co., and Mana Foods grocery. Stock up for Road to Hana here.
💵 Money
Carry cash ($100–150 in small bills) Essential for: roadside banana bread stands, shave ice carts, beach parking, tips. Most major spots accept cards but cash wins at local roadside stops.
Costco saves serious money Costco Iwilei (Honolulu, near airport): Snorkel gear (~$25), sunscreen, snacks on arrival day.
Costco Maui (Kahului, near OGG): Water, snacks, sunscreen — resort prices are brutal.
Souvenirs — buy at the grocery store Kona coffee, macadamia nuts, Maui Brewing Co. drinks — Costco or Safeway/Foodland have the same items 40% cheaper than resort gift shops.
🎒 Packing Essentials
🧥 Haleakalā summit — it's cold up there 40–50°F at sunrise. Bring a proper jacket, gloves, and warm layers. Non-negotiable — don't let the 80°F beach weather fool you.
☀️ Sun protection Reef-safe mineral sunscreen only — required by law at Hanauma Bay. Hawaii's sun is more intense than the mainland. Add a rashguard for snorkelling.
👟 Footwear Water shoes for lava rocks (Wai'anapanapa), hiking shoes for Diamond Head & Haleakalā, flip-flops for beach. Three pairs covers everything.
📱 Practical
Cell coverage on Road to Hana Spotty to nonexistent once you leave Paia. Download offline Google Maps for the entire Hana Hwy before you leave Wailea in the morning.
